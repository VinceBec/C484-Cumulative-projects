The Author

Catch-22 was written by Joseph Heller starting in 1953 and finally published in 1961. The novel is his best known work, with Heller's work process being that we would not begin to work until he though up the first and last lines of the story. Apparently one morning he thought up the line "It was love at first sight. The first time he saw the chaplain, [Yossarian] fell madly in love with him." and decided to use that for the opening of the novel.

Life and War

Heller was born in 1923 in Brooklyn, New York. Heller liked to write early in life and sent a Story to the New York Times about the Russian Invasion of Finland (They rejected it). He later worked as a blacksmith's apprentice, a messenger boy, and a filing clerk.

World War II started and in 1942, at age 19, Heller joined the U.S. Army Air Corps. Heller flew 60 missions and upon coming home many were saying he was a hero. Heller didn't think much of it as he was he mainly did "milk runs", or missions that were expected to be completely safe.

After the war, Heller went on to study English at the University of Southern California, New York University, and Columbia University. He eventually recieved his M.A. in English in 1949. Other than Catch-22, Heller would also finish writing 6 more novels, 1 play, 3 screenplays, and around 7 short stories. Heller passed away in December of 1999.
